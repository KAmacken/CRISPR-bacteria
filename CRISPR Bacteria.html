What is CRISPR?

CRISPR is a specialized region of DNA with a two specific traits. It stands for clustered regularly interspaced palindromic repeats. CRISPR-Cas9 is a gene-editing tool that has the possibility of turning on and off specified genes and gives the biomedical world the chance to modify the human genome once more research is conducted.
